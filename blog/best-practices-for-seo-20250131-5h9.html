Best Practices for SEO: Because Google Ain't Gonna Rank You for Free

January 9, 2024 • 12 min read

Mike Johnson

SEO Specialist

Alright, let's talk SEO. You know, that thing everyone says you need to do but no one really explains how to do without sounding like a robot? Yeah, that. SEO (Search Engine Optimization) is basically your website's way of saying, "Hey Google, I exist, and I'm awesome!" But here's the kicker: Google doesn't care about your feelings. It's all about playing by the rules—well, kinda.

1. Keywords Are Your BFFs (But Don't Be Weird About It)

Keywords are like the breadcrumbs that lead Google to your site. But here's the thing: don't stuff them everywhere like you're trying to win a game of SEO bingo. It's not 2010 anymore, and Google's smarter than that.

  • Use keywords naturally in your titles, headers, and content
  • Think of it like seasoning food—just enough to make it tasty
  • Avoid keyword stuffing at all costs

2. Content Is King, but Quality Is the Crown

You've probably heard "content is king" a million times. But here's the twist: crappy content is like a king with no pants—embarrassing and ineffective.

Pro Tip: Content Quality

Write stuff people actually want to read. If your blog post is drier than a saltine cracker, no one's sticking around.

3. Speed Matters (No One Likes a Slowpoke)

Imagine this: you're trying to watch a cat video, but the page takes 10 seconds to load. You'd bounce faster than a trampoline, right? Same goes for your site.

  • Optimize your images
  • Use a decent hosting provider
  • Minify your code

"SEO isn't just about pleasing Google; it's about pleasing your visitors too."

4. Mobile-Friendly Is Non-Negotiable

If your site looks like a hot mess on mobile, you're basically telling half your audience to peace out. And Google's not a fan either.

5. Stay Updated (Because Google Changes the Rules)

Google's algorithm updates are like plot twists in a soap opera—unpredictable and sometimes dramatic. Keep an eye on industry news and adapt accordingly.
